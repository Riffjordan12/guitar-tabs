NOTES

  on the record, Buffy starts by picking notes but ends strumming;
  it is played at a much faster pace (Allegro - quickly) than later
  recordings by others (which are more often Adagio - slowly)

  recorded key is A - here it's transposed to G - ie to play in original key,
  place the capo on the 2nd fret; tho this arrangement follows the original
  closely, it doesn't (and wasn't meant to) follow it exactly; however the
  lyrics, vocal phrasing and melody should not be affected by any changes

  format : verse 1, verse 2, chorus, verse 3, chorus, verse 4
  the lyrics in verse 1 are repeated in verse 4

  the verses are similar to each other, tho the arrangements at the
  end of the first lines (from Dm thru E7 to Am) are more ornate in
  the last 2 verses; the 2 choruses are the same

  the first note of the bar (usually the bass note of an
  arpeggio) is generally held for the duration of the bar 

  Bars 1-4 (intro) : on the original recording the instrumental
  accompaniment starts on bar 5 (after the start of the vocal)

  Bars 5-8 : (tip: keep the 4th finger on 3rd fret of 2nd string)

  Bar 10 : barre 1st fret of just the 2nd & 3rd strings with the 1st
  finger; it's a bit of a stretch with 2nd finger on the 4th string
  and the 4th finger on the 6th string, but well worth practising as
  it maintains the lovely downward octave run, A Ab G, thru bars 9-11
  (tip: keep the 2nd finger on 2nd fret of 4th string bars 9 thru 11)

  Bars 37-50 : standard chord shapes using 1st finger as barre

  Bar 49 : the original continues on Em7, but here it changes to A7
  (a bar earlier) which suits the arrangement better
  (nb equivalent Bar 81 in the 2nd chorus is as the recording - A7)

  Bars 100-102 : standard chord shapes using 1st finger as barre
